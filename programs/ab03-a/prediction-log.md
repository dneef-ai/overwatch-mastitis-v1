# AB03-A Prediction Log — Rumen H₂ Sink

---

## Phase 1: Pathfinder

### Prediction 1: Reductive Acetogenesis Is the Dominant Unaccounted Sink
- **Prediction:** Under >60% methanogenesis inhibition in vivo, reductive acetogenesis accounts for >25% of the "missing hydrogen" identified in the Ungerfeld 2015 meta-analysis. The reason it appears "missing" in prior studies is that acetate from acetogenesis (4H₂ + 2CO₂ → acetate) is indistinguishable from fermentative acetate without isotope tracing.
- **Test:** KE#1 — ¹³C-CO₂ pulse experiment in cannulated cattle under 3-NOP treatment. If ¹³C enrichment in rumen acetate increases significantly during methanogenesis inhibition vs. control, reductive acetogenesis is active and quantifiable.
- **If TRUE:** Acetogen enhancement becomes the primary AB03 strategy. Direct-fed acetogens, conditions favoring *Candidatus* Faecousia and other rumen acetogens, and potentially engineered high-throughput acetogens become top-priority candidates. The portfolio pivots toward biological interventions rather than chemical electron acceptors.
- **If FALSE:** Reductive acetogenesis is not a viable sink even at elevated H₂. The portfolio pivots toward exogenous electron acceptor supply (fumarate, novel acceptors) or engineered organisms with entirely new H₂-consuming pathways. The hindgut precedent for acetogenesis is irrelevant to the rumen.

### Prediction 2: The Kinetic Bottleneck Is Population Size, Not Enzyme Kinetics
- **Prediction:** If alternative H₂-consuming bacteria (Prevotella, Selenomonas, acetogens) are supplemented as direct-fed microbials at >10⁸ CFU/day alongside 3-NOP, dissolved H₂ concentrations will decrease by >40% compared to 3-NOP alone, and propionate molar proportion will increase by >5 percentage points.
- **Test:** In vitro RUSITEC experiment: 3-NOP alone vs. 3-NOP + high-dose mixed inoculum of Prevotella brevis + Selenomonas ruminantium + Eubacterium limosum. Measure dissolved H₂, VFA profile, and total VFA production over 14 days.
- **If TRUE:** The bottleneck is indeed organism population size. AB03 strategy focuses on live microbial supplementation — the question becomes formulation, delivery, and persistence in the rumen. This is a direct-fed microbial product opportunity.
- **If FALSE:** The bottleneck is not population size but intrinsic enzymatic capacity or ecological constraints (predation, competition for non-H₂ substrates). Strategy shifts to engineering organisms with higher Vmax hydrogenases or to non-biological electron acceptor delivery.

### Prediction 3: The Dose-Response for Productivity Loss Is Non-Linear
- **Prediction:** There exists a critical dissolved H₂ threshold between 20-40 μM above which fiber degradation decreases sharply (>15% NDF degradation loss). Below this threshold, the rumen self-corrects with minimal productivity impact. This explains why 30% methane inhibition is well tolerated but 80% inhibition shows variable and sometimes severe effects.
- **Test:** Dose-escalation study with 3-NOP in cannulated cattle: measure dissolved H₂ continuously and correlate with NDF degradation (nylon bag technique) at each dose level. Plot the dose-response curve for dissolved H₂ vs. fiber degradation.
- **If TRUE:** AB03 must keep dissolved H₂ below the threshold (20-40 μM) even during aggressive methanogenesis inhibition. The intervention is quantitatively defined: sink enough H₂ to stay below threshold. This constrains the minimum capacity required of any AB03 product.
- **If FALSE:** The relationship is linear, meaning any H₂ reduction helps proportionally but there is no safe operating window. The intervention must aim for near-complete H₂ disposal rather than threshold maintenance.

### Prediction 4: 3-NOP + Fumarate Combination Will Show Synergistic Effects In Vivo
- **Prediction:** Co-supplementation of 3-NOP (150 mg/kg DM) + sodium fumarate (2% DM) in dairy cows will reduce methane by >40% while maintaining or improving milk yield compared to 3-NOP alone, because fumarate provides the electron acceptor that redirects H₂ into propionate (glucogenic VFA) rather than allowing it to accumulate.
- **Test:** Randomized controlled trial: 3-NOP alone vs. 3-NOP + fumarate vs. fumarate alone vs. control, in lactating dairy cows (n=12/group), 8-week trial. Measure CH₄ emissions, milk yield/composition, VFA profiles, and dissolved H₂.
- **If TRUE:** The combination product is the AB03 minimum viable product — a dual-action formulation combining methanogenesis inhibition with electron acceptor supplementation. IP position: the combination claim, not the individual components. Commercial path: co-formulation with existing 3-NOP products (Bovaer).
- **If FALSE:** Fumarate's in vivo efficacy in cattle is insufficient (consistent with the meta-analysis showing non-significant effects in cattle). AB03 must pursue higher-potency electron acceptors or biological solutions rather than fumarate.

### Prediction 5: Formate Is the Missing Electron Carrier
- **Prediction:** Under methanogenesis inhibition, formate concentrations in rumen fluid increase by >5-fold compared to control, and formate accounts for >15% of the "missing hydrogen" pool. Formate serves as a liquid-phase electron carrier that buffers H₂ concentration by converting between H₂ + CO₂ ⇌ formate.
- **Test:** Time-course measurement of rumen formate concentration (ion chromatography) in cannulated cattle under control vs. 3-NOP treatment, sampled every 2 hours for 24 hours post-feeding.
- **If TRUE:** Formate metabolism is a major and previously underappreciated component of the H₂ disposal system. Interventions targeting formate-utilizing organisms or formate-to-propionate conversion become relevant. The standard hydrogen balance equations (which often omit formate) are incomplete.
- **If FALSE:** Formate does not accumulate significantly, meaning it is rapidly consumed or the H₂-formate equilibrium favors H₂ under inhibited conditions. The missing hydrogen must be elsewhere (biomass, reductive acetogenesis, or genuinely lost).
