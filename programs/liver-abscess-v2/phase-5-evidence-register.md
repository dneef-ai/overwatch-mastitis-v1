## Evidence Standards

Every claim in this register is tagged with:
- **PMID/DOI:** The specific citation
- **Evidence tier:** [ESTABLISHED] (multiple independent confirmations), [MODERATE] (peer-reviewed, limited replication), [PRELIMINARY] (single study / preprint / conference), [INFERRED] (logical deduction from related data), [COMPUTATIONAL] (in silico analysis only)
- **Species/model:** The species or system in which the evidence was generated
- **Replication:** Independent lab confirmation status
- **Key finding:** Exact finding or quote relevant to the portfolio

A reviewer checking any 3 citations at random must find them correct.

### B2: V2/LktC -- Acyltransferase Assay (LEAD EXPERIMENT, Rank 1)

| # | Citation | Evidence Tier | Species | Replication | Key Finding |
|---|----------|--------------|---------|-------------|-------------|
| B2.1 | Narayanan et al. 2001, PMID: 11500416 | ESTABLISHED | In vitro (molecular) | Confirmed | lktBAC operon has same three-gene organization as RTX operons. LktC (145 aa) is annotated with conflicting domains (histidine kinase vs. acyltransferase). Gene order homology to RTX systems suggests acyltransferase function. |
| B2.2 | Argus v9 Surveyor analysis | [COMPUTATIONAL] | In silico (BLAST, CDD, ESMFold) | Computational only | Histidine kinase annotation is WRONG (zero kinase domains on CDD, 96-100% identity to characterized LktC). CCHH motif present. YbjN domain. Actual function unresolved between acyltransferase, zinc metalloenzyme, and chaperone. Druggability: MODERATE-TO-FAVORABLE (8,317 A^3 cavity in ESMFold model). |
| B2.3 | RTX toxin biology (general) | ESTABLISHED | Multiple bacterial species | Extensive | In canonical RTX toxin systems, the C gene product is an acyltransferase (e.g., HlyC for E. coli hemolysin) that attaches acyl-linked fatty acids to internal lysine residues. Without acylation, RTX toxins are inactive. Catalytic mechanism involves conserved histidine. Active site structurally distinct from host acyltransferases. |
| B2.4 | Vulcan first-principles analysis (Phase 3) | [ANALYTICAL] | -- | Quarantined independent analysis | LktA has NO cysteine residues, meaning no disulfide bonds. Protein folding and activity entirely dependent on post-translational modifications (acylation) and calcium binding. This makes acylation more likely to be essential than for typical cysteine-containing proteins. |

**Critical caveat:** LktA has NO sequence homology to RTX toxins. The acyltransferase hypothesis rests entirely on operon organization analogy. Reaper correctly identifies that gene order homology does not prove functional homology. The $5-10K recombinant assay is BINARY: confirms or permanently kills this target.

## SECTION F: Evidence Integrity Notes

1. **No citation in this register has been verified by checking the original paper.** All citations are as reported by the 9-agent pipeline (Pathfinder, Sapper, Forge, Surveyor, Reaper, Board) and their external model panels. A reviewer should verify any 3 citations at random before relying on this register.

2. **PMIDs and DOIs are provided where available.** Some citations (particularly early Kanoe & Iwaki 1987, Saginala 1996/1997 challenge studies) have PMIDs but not DOIs. Modern citations generally have both.

3. **"Established" evidence tier** means multiple independent groups have confirmed the finding. For F. necrophorum-specific claims, "established" often means the finding is widely cited and accepted, but may originate from a small number of labs (primarily KSU/Nagaraja). The distinction between "widely accepted" and "independently replicated" is important and is flagged where relevant.

4. **Computational evidence** from Argus v9/v15 (BLAST, CDD, ESMFold predictions) is tagged as [COMPUTATIONAL] and should not be treated as equivalent to wet-lab validation. Structure predictions (ESMFold, AlphaFold) have confidence scores (pLDDT) but remain predictions.
