# Prediction Log — AB03-D (H₂-Independent Productivity Gains)

---

## Phase 1: Pathfinder

### Prediction 1: The H₂-Independent Energy Fraction
- **Prediction:** When methanogenesis is inhibited while H₂ is simultaneously disposed of (3-NOP + fumarate/malate), the productivity gain will be <1.5% ECM — less than half the total productivity effect observed with 3-NOP alone (+3-6.5% ECM).
- **Test:** KE#1 factorial experiment: 3-NOP + H₂ sink vs H₂ sink alone. Compare ECM yield between groups.
- **If TRUE (H₂-independent fraction is <1.5%):** AB03-D is closed as a discovery program. The commercially meaningful productivity effect is H₂-mediated. All resources redirect to AB03-A/B/C H₂ sink optimization.
- **If FALSE (H₂-independent fraction is >1.5%):** AB03-D is validated. The specific H₂-independent mechanism(s) driving the effect become priority targets for the next pipeline phase. Energy budget and passage-rate mechanisms move to the front of the queue.

### Prediction 2: The Passage Rate Effect
- **Prediction:** Cattle receiving 3-NOP will show increased mean retention time (MRT) of particulate digesta by 5-15% compared to controls, and this MRT increase will correlate positively with NDF digestibility improvement.
- **Test:** Cr-mordanted NDF passage rate measurement in KE#1 factorial. Compare Group 2 (3-NOP alone) vs Group 1 (control) and Group 4 (3-NOP + H₂ sink) vs Group 3 (H₂ sink alone).
- **If TRUE:** The Dittmann (2016) methane-motility-MRT mechanism is confirmed in lactating cattle and contributes a genuine H₂-independent digestibility benefit. This could be enhanced by managing eructation/motility signals independently.
- **If FALSE (MRT unchanged or decreased):** The Dittmann observation was specific to non-lactating cows, gas insufflation conditions, or was a statistical artifact. The physical gas-volume mechanism is dead as an H₂-independent pathway.

### Prediction 3: Protozoal Community Invariance
- **Prediction:** Protozoal populations will remain unaffected by 3-NOP at standard doses (60-80 mg/kg DM) regardless of H₂ status, confirming that defaunation-like benefits cannot be accessed through methanogenesis inhibition alone.
- **Test:** Protozoal counts and community composition (18S) in all four KE#1 groups. Compare across treatments.
- **If TRUE:** Protozoa are genuinely insensitive to MCR inhibition, likely because endosymbiotic methanogens are physically protected. Any future protozoal-productivity strategy requires a separate intervention (targeted antiprotozoal, not anti-methanogenic).
- **If FALSE (protozoa are affected at sustained treatment):** There may be a delayed or dose-dependent protozoal response that short-term studies missed. This would open a major new H₂-independent mechanism — partial defaunation via methanogenesis inhibition — with +30% microbial protein flow as the prize.

### Prediction 4: The Van Gastelen Effect Will Not Fully Replicate
- **Prediction:** The +6.5% ECM yield observed in van Gastelen et al. (2024) will not replicate at this magnitude in the factorial KE#1 experiment. The 3-NOP-alone group (Group 2) will show +1.5-4.0% ECM over control — positive but smaller than 6.5%.
- **Test:** Direct measurement in the KE#1 3-NOP group vs control.
- **If TRUE (+1.5-4.0% ECM):** The van Gastelen result included favorable conditions (breed, management, diet composition, or measurement period effects). The true effect of 3-NOP on productivity is modest and primarily H₂-mediated. AB03-D's H₂-independent fraction is even smaller than the already-small prediction.
- **If FALSE (>5% ECM replicates):** There is a robust, large productivity effect of 3-NOP that demands mechanistic explanation. If the H₂-independent component (from Prediction 1) is still small, then H₂-mediated propionate-shift energy capture is more powerful than expected — which redirects AB03 but validates the commercial case for methane inhibitors as productivity tools, not just emissions tools.

### Prediction 5: Microbial Protein Synthesis Will Not Increase
- **Prediction:** Microbial protein flow to the duodenum (measured by purine derivatives) will NOT be significantly increased in any 3-NOP treatment group compared to its respective control, confirming the negative results from Ungerfeld (2019, 2020, 2022).
- **Test:** Purine derivative excretion (urinary allantoin and uric acid) as proxy for duodenal microbial protein flow in all KE#1 groups.
- **If TRUE:** The MPS-efficiency hypothesis is definitively dead for methanogenesis inhibition. The energy freed by reducing methane does NOT flow to microbial growth — it either stays in VFAs or is lost. Nitrogen metabolism is not an H₂-independent productivity mechanism.
- **If FALSE (MPS increases):** Previous negative results were artifacts of short-term in vitro systems or nitrogen-deficient diets. If MPS increases in well-nourished in vivo cattle under sustained inhibition, this reopens a major H₂-independent mechanism and changes the amino acid supply story for dairy formulation.
