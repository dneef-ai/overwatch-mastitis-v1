**Program:** Cryptosporidiosis | **Partner:** Cargill (Animal Nutrition & Health)

## Executive Summary

Reaper delivered a clean kill report: 11 survived, 19 wounded, 12 killed. The 6-model external panel then stress-tested those verdicts. After synthesizing all feedback, applying the devil's advocate inversion, and force-ranking survivors, the Board delivers three outputs:

1. **Priority De-Risk Sequence:** Three experiments, sequenced to resolve the biggest unknowns first.
2. **KE#1 Recommendation:** The drug-vs-IFN-gamma trial remains the right KE#1, but with modifications.
3. **Cargill Portfolio Framing:** What Cargill can own vs. what requires a pharma partner.

**The hard truth this portfolio faces:** The two strongest assets (EDI048, AN7973) are not Cargill's to develop. The assets Cargill can develop independently (I3C, beta-glucan, lactose-reduced MR, colostrum, ORS) cannot pass the 70% test without an antiparasitic backbone. The Board's job is to sequence decisions so that Cargill invests its first dollar in resolving whether that backbone can be accessed --- and builds its own product layer in parallel.

## Step 2: Devil's Advocate Inversion

For each SURVIVED target, the Board now argues the opposite position.

### A1. EDI048 (CpPI4K) --- Argue for KILL

**Compound contamination check:** EDI048 is in the portfolio because Novartis developed a specific compound. Would PI4K be a top target if no compound existed? YES --- PI4K is a core membrane trafficking kinase, CRISPR-unable-to-KO (presumably essential), and the biological logic for blocking segmentation/egress is sound regardless of compound availability. EDI048 passes the contamination test.

**Alternative explanation:** Could Stage 3 be addressed differently? YES --- AN7973 (CPSF3) addresses the same stage via a different mechanism. Lapaquistat/GSH axis (B6) addresses it via host redox manipulation. EDI048 is NOT the only path to Stage 3, which reduces concentration risk.

**"Cargill already knows this" test:** Cargill is NOT a pharmaceutical company. They do not develop kinase inhibitors. The relevant question is whether Novartis already knows this --- and the answer is yes, Novartis is actively developing EDI048 (Phase 2 recruiting, NCT07249463). Cargill's value-add is zero for compound development. Cargill's value-add is in (a) the veterinary clinical trial infrastructure, (b) the field deployment via feed/nutrition channels, and (c) the animal nutrition products that surround the antiparasitic. The pitch to Novartis is: "We provide the veterinary development path and market access you don't have."

**The strongest kill argument:** EDI048 is potentially STATIC. If it arrests parasites without killing them, it is a more expensive halofuginone with the same fundamental limitation. The absence of time-kill data is not a minor gap --- it is THE gap. A static PI4K inhibitor at $15-30/treatment requiring 7+ days of dosing is commercially dead.

**Board conclusion:** SURVIVED, but KE#1 (cidal-vs-static determination) must precede all other investment. If static, EDI048 drops to combination-partner status and the entire de-risk sequence changes.

### A2. AN7973 (CPSF3) --- Argue for KILL

**Compound contamination check:** AN7973 is a tool compound, not a clinical candidate. Would CPSF3 be a top target without AN7973? YES --- CPSF3 is an essential mRNA processing enzyme with structural characterization and dual-species activity. But the benzoxaborole chemistry class IS the approach to CPSF3 --- without boron-zinc chelation, the mechanism doesn't exist. So AN7973 is compound-contaminated in the sense that the target and the chemistry are inseparable.

**Alternative explanation:** No. AN7973 is the ONLY cidal, multi-stage, immunity-independent compound with calf efficacy data. There is no alternative path to this pharmacological profile.

**"Cargill already knows this" test:** Cargill does not know this. This is cutting-edge parasitology published in Nature Communications and Science Translational Medicine. The value-add opportunity for Cargill is identical to EDI048: veterinary clinical infrastructure + market access.

**The strongest kill argument:** The 25 mg/kg dose is a potential commercial killer. For a 40 kg calf over 7 days = 7 grams of a boron-containing synthetic compound. Benzoxaborole synthesis is not cheap. The tool compound was never optimized for COGS. If dose optimization can't get below 5-10 mg/kg, the economics may not work at veterinary price points. Additionally, benzoxaborole stability in the acidic abomasum is uncertain --- the calf trial showed it WORKS, but we don't know how much drug survived GI transit.

**Board conclusion:** SURVIVED. The cidal + multi-stage + immunity-independent profile is unique and irreplaceable. The dose/cost concern is real but premature to kill on --- dose optimization is standard. GI stability testing is cheap.

### B15. EDI048 + AN7973 Combination --- Argue for KILL

**The strongest kill argument:** This is a fantasy. Two novel compounds from different owners, neither with veterinary approval, requiring FDA-CVM combination product approval (individual approvals first, then superiority proof). The regulatory timeline is 10-15 years and >$10M. No partner will fund this until both individual compounds are approved. This is not a near-term product concept; it is an endpoint that may never arrive.

**Board conclusion:** SURVIVED as a long-horizon strategic goal, but REMOVED from the near-term portfolio framing. Cargill should pursue access to each compound independently. Combination off-label use is possible without formal combination approval.

### B12. Colostrum Supplementation --- Argue for KILL

**The strongest kill argument:** Every dairy farm already uses colostrum. "Standardized high-quality colostrum product" is table stakes for Cargill, not a crypto intervention. Effect on crypto is modest at best. This is a product Cargill should sell regardless of crypto --- including it as a "crypto portfolio asset" is padding.

**Board conclusion:** RETAINED as standard-of-care but NOT counted as a crypto-specific intervention for the 70% test. This is nutritional baseline, not therapeutic.

### B13. Glycine/Glutamine ORS --- Argue for KILL

**The strongest kill argument:** ORS is supportive care. It does not kill the parasite. It does not prevent infection. It reduces dehydration severity. Every veterinarian already uses ORS. An "optimized" ORS is a marginal improvement on an already-universal intervention.

**Board conclusion:** RETAINED as supportive care. NOT counted toward 70% test. Cargill product opportunity regardless of crypto program.

### D8. Lactose-Reduced Milk Replacer --- Argue for KILL

**The strongest kill argument:** Lactose provides 40% of calories. Reducing it without careful caloric replacement risks worsening growth deficits in already-compromised calves. No crypto-specific data exists. The "secondary lactase deficiency" rationale is plausible but unproven. This survives because Cargill can make it, not because biology supports it.

**Board conclusion:** WOUNDED. Retain only if trial design includes caloric-balanced reformulation and weight gain as co-primary endpoint.

### EXT-7. Zinc --- Argue for KILL

**The strongest kill argument:** WHO recommends zinc for human childhood diarrhea. That recommendation is for a different species, different pathogen, different disease context. No crypto-calf data. Cross-species generalization from human pediatric practice to neonatal calf protozoan infection is exactly the kind of low-quality extrapolation the portfolio should avoid.

**Board conclusion:** DEMOTED. Stays as background supplementation, not counted toward coverage, no de-risk investment.

### EXT-9. MCFAs --- Argue for KILL

**The strongest kill argument:** MCFAs disrupt membranes of extracellular sporozoites/merozoites during a transit window measured in minutes. Once the parasite is in its epicellular niche (the vast majority of its lifecycle), MCFAs are irrelevant. Mixed calf results confirm this --- marginal at best.

**Board conclusion:** DEMOTED. Same as zinc and beta-glucan: background feed additive, not a portfolio asset.

### D4. Mucoadhesive Nanoparticles --- Argue for KILL

**The strongest kill argument (per GPT-5.4):** A platform without a payload is not a candidate. Crypto-induced mucus depletion undermines the core retention mechanism. Manufacturing cost is nontrivial. This survives only because it is a "concept."

**Board conclusion:** KILLED as independent candidate. Reconsider ONLY when a specific drug needs improved delivery.

## Step 4: Board Decision

### A. Priority De-Risk Sequence --- "Fund 3, Which 3?"

The three experiments are sequenced to maximize information value per dollar spent. Each experiment's result changes what happens next.

**Experiment 1: EDI048 Cidal-vs-Static Determination**

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **What** | Time-kill curve assay of EDI048 against *C. parvum* in bovine ALI intestinal organoids. Multiple concentrations (0.1x, 1x, 10x EC50). Measure parasite viability at 6h, 12h, 24h, 48h, 72h. Include washout: remove drug at 48h, measure recrudescence at 7 days. |
| **Why first** | This is the single most portfolio-restructuring experiment. If EDI048 is CIDAL, it is unambiguously the #1 compound and the entire de-risk sequence accelerates. If STATIC, EDI048 drops to combination-partner status and AN7973 becomes the sole backbone. Every dollar spent on EDI048 before knowing cidal-vs-static is a dollar risked on the halofuginone failure mode. |
| **Cost** | $8,000-12,000 (organoid culture + time-kill + recrudescence) |
| **Timeline** | 4-6 weeks |
| **GO** | >2 log reduction in viable parasites at 48h; no recrudescence at 7 days post-washout. |
| **KILL** | <1 log reduction at 48h; rapid recrudescence within 3 days of washout. This kills EDI048 as a standalone and forces the combination strategy. |

**Experiment 2: AN7973 Independent Calf Replication + Resistance Selection**

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **What** | Two parallel studies: (a) Independent neonatal calf efficacy trial (n=8-10 per group: vehicle, AN7973 at 15 and 25 mg/kg, halofuginone comparator). Challenge with field isolate (IIa subtype), not just IOWA-ATCC. Primary endpoints: oocyst clearance (qPCR), diarrhea days, weight at day 30. (b) In vitro resistance selection: expose *C. parvum* to sub-inhibitory AN7973 concentrations in ALI organoid system for serial passages. Sequence CPSF3 at each passage. |
| **Why second** | The 6-model panel unanimously flagged single-lab dependency on both backbone compounds. Independent replication is mandatory before committing >$100K. The resistance selection experiment resolves whether AN7973's favorable resistance profile (6 AN3661 mutations do NOT confer AN7973 resistance) holds under direct selective pressure. This is THE unresolved question for AN7973. |
| **Cost** | $60,000-80,000 (calf trial: ~$50K; resistance selection: ~$15-20K) |
| **Timeline** | 10-14 weeks (calf trial) + 8-12 weeks (resistance selection, can run in parallel) |
| **GO** | >90% oocyst reduction confirmed by independent lab; no resistance mutations detected in 10+ passages at sub-IC50. |
| **KILL** | Efficacy <50% in independent lab (suggesting original results were inflated); or resistance mutations detected within 5 passages (MetRS-like rapid resistance). |

**Experiment 3: Anti-P23 IgY Independent Replication + Subtype Cross-Reactivity**

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **What** | Two components: (a) Independent neonatal calf trial with anti-P23 IgY (reproduce Cho et al. 2025 protocol, n=8-10 per group). (b) ELISA/SPR binding assay of anti-P23 IgY against recombinant P23 from IIa (North American) and IId (Asian/European) subtypes. If binding is equivalent, subtype concern is resolved. If binding drops >10-fold for IId, the product is geographically limited. |
| **Why third** | This is Cargill's ONLY proprietary anti-crypto asset. Egg-based IgY production, spray-dried oral delivery, feed-additive formulation --- all core Cargill capabilities. If P23 IgY replicates and cross-reacts, Cargill has a product it can develop entirely in-house without licensing from pharma. This is the experiment that determines whether Cargill has a proprietary crypto product or is entirely dependent on pharma partnerships. |
| **Cost** | $40,000-50,000 (calf trial: ~$35K; recombinant P23 production + binding assays: ~$10-15K) |
| **Timeline** | 10-14 weeks |
| **GO** | Significant diarrhea reduction + oocyst reduction confirmed independently; IgY binds IIa and IId P23 with <5-fold affinity difference. |
| **KILL** | No significant effect in independent trial; or IgY binding to IId P23 is >10-fold weaker (geographic limitation makes the product commercially unviable for global markets). |

**Total de-risk cost: $108,000-142,000**
**Total timeline: 14-18 weeks (Experiments 2 and 3 run in parallel after Experiment 1)**

### B. KE#1 Recommendation

Pathfinder proposed a $15-20K drug-vs-IFN-gamma controlled infection trial as the portfolio-restructuring experiment. The Board's assessment:

**The original KE#1 is still the right strategic question, but the Board recommends splitting it into two phases.**

**Phase A (proceed immediately, $8-12K):** Experiment 1 above --- the EDI048 time-kill assay. This resolves the cidal-vs-static question in 4-6 weeks at one-tenth the cost of the full KE#1 trial. If EDI048 is cidal, the full drug-vs-IFN-gamma trial becomes lower priority (the drug doesn't need immune help). If EDI048 is static, the full trial becomes HIGHER priority (immune cooperation is the only path to cure with available compounds).

**Phase B (conditional on Phase A result, $15-20K):** If EDI048 is STATIC, run the full 4-arm trial (vehicle / EDI048 / IFN-gamma / EDI048+IFN-gamma). This now becomes urgent: it tells us whether immune supplementation can convert a static drug into a curative regimen. The answer restructures whether Cargill's immune-modulating products (beta-glucan, I3C, colostrum) have a pharmacological role or are just background nutrition.

**If EDI048 is CIDAL:** Phase B is deprioritized. A cidal drug that works without immune cooperation doesn't need the IFN-gamma trial. Resources shift to Experiments 2 and 3.

## Board Decision Summary
