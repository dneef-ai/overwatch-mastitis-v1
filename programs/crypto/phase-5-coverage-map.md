## Step 1: Tractability Classification

Each disease stage from Pathfinder's disease map is classified as TRACTABLE or NON-TRACTABLE for Cargill. Tractable means a pharmaceutical, biological, or device intervention is plausible and could be developed or licensed by Cargill. Non-tractable means the stage is addressed by management, genomics, or infrastructure investment that falls outside a product portfolio.

| Disease Stage | Classification | Rationale |
|---------------|---------------|-----------|
| **Stage 0: Upstream Susceptibility** | **PARTIALLY TRACTABLE** | Immune immaturity is biological and not pharmacologically acceleratable. Passive immunity (colostrum, IgY) is tractable as a product. Microbiome immaturity is not tractable as a crypto-specific intervention. Scored as 50% tractable: the passive antibody component is a product; the immune maturation timeline is biology. |
| **Stage 1: Exposure and Ingestion** | **NON-TRACTABLE** | Environmental oocyst control is management (housing, hygiene, all-in/all-out). Oocyst disinfectant resistance defeats all standard chemical approaches. At R0 = 5-15, no management intervention reduces exposure below the infectious dose threshold (10-30 oocysts). Not a product opportunity for Cargill. |
| **Stage 2: Excystation and Invasion** | **TRACTABLE** | Anti-reinvasion products (anti-P23 IgY, VHH nanobodies) are biologically feasible and commercially viable as oral supplements/feed additives. Cargill can manufacture, distribute, and sell these. |
| **Stage 3: Intracellular Development** | **TRACTABLE** | The central stage. Direct-kill antiparasitic drugs (EDI048, AN7973) can penetrate the epicellular niche from the luminal side. Requires licensing from pharma, but the products are developable as oral veterinary formulations. |
| **Stage 4: Host Immune Response** | **PARTIALLY TRACTABLE** | No validated immune potentiator exists for neonatal calf crypto. IL-22/I3C inducer is hypothesis-stage only and has been downgraded by the Board. Scored as 25% tractable: concept exists but no validated product, and the neonatal immune maturation timeline is fundamentally biological. |
| **Stage 5: Intestinal Pathology** | **PARTIALLY TRACTABLE** | Supportive care (ORS, anti-secretory agents) is tractable as a product category. But supportive care does not address the parasite or the underlying pathology --- it mitigates dehydration. The 34 kg growth deficit at 6 months is NOT addressed by any current intervention. Scored as 50% tractable: dehydration management is a product; mucosal repair and growth recovery remain unaddressed. |
| **Stage 6: Shedding and Transmission** | **TRACTABLE (secondary)** | Shedding reduction is a secondary benefit of Stage 3 antiparasitic treatment, not a primary product target. At R0 = 5-15, shedding reduction must exceed 80-93% to meaningfully impact herd-level transmission. Tractable only through the antiparasitic backbone. |
| **Stage 7: Resolution or Chronicity** | **PARTIALLY TRACTABLE** | Nutritional support (lactose-reduced MR, optimized ORS, colostrum) during recovery is tractable. But the persistent growth deficit and lifetime productivity impact are fundamentally driven by the severity and duration of the acute infection --- which depends on the antiparasitic backbone. Scored as 40% tractable. |

| Disease Stage | Tractable Weight (%) | Basis |
|---------------|---------------------|-------|
| Stage 0 (tractable portion) | 5% | Passive immunity modulates severity but does not prevent infection. Colostrum/IgY reduces diarrhea by ~2 days. Modest contribution. |
| Stage 1 | 0% (NON-TRACTABLE) | Management only. Not counted. |
| Stage 2: Invasion | 10% | Anti-reinvasion blocks sporozoite/merozoite attachment during extracellular transit. Cannot stop initial infection or intracellular stages. Value as combination partner reducing autoinfection rate. |
| Stage 3: Intracellular Development | **45%** | This is the rate-limiting stage. The epicellular niche + autoinfection cycle drive the entire disease. Pathfinder, Sapper, and the Board all identify Stage 3 as the dominant determinant of disease severity. If intracellular parasite burden is controlled, clinical disease resolves. |
| Stage 4 (tractable portion) | 5% | No validated product exists. IL-22/I3C is exploratory only. Small weight reflects the absence of a viable intervention, not the biological unimportance of immunity. |
| Stage 5 (tractable portion) | 15% | Supportive care prevents deaths from dehydration. Meaningful clinical impact (calves that receive appropriate ORS survive). But purely symptomatic --- does not affect parasite burden, shedding, or long-term growth. |
| Stage 6: Shedding | 10% | Secondary to Stage 3 antiparasitic. Shedding reduction has herd-level benefit over time but minimal individual-animal benefit beyond what Stage 3 treatment provides. |
| Stage 7 (tractable portion) | 10% | Recovery-phase nutrition supports weight gain after acute infection. But the 34 kg deficit at 6 months is fundamentally driven by acute disease severity, not recovery nutrition. |

## Step 5: 70% Test Result

### VERDICT: THE PORTFOLIO DOES NOT PASS THE 70% TEST.

**Honest assessment:**

- **Realistic scenario (A):** 46.3% coverage. Fails by 23.7 percentage points.
- **Optimistic scenario (B):** 68% coverage. Borderline fail even with every assumption maximized.
- **Cargill-alone scenario (C):** 15.75%. Not a portfolio.

### Why It Fails

1. **Stage 3 dominates (45% of tractable pathology) and only two compounds address it, both requiring pharma licensing.** Even with both compounds accessible and cidal, the maximum realistic contribution is ~21% of total coverage. To pass the 70% test, Stage 3 coverage would need to be ~35%+ --- which requires sterilizing cure, not the 90-99% reduction demonstrated.

2. **Stage 4 (immune response) has zero coverage.** No validated immune potentiator exists. The neonatal immune maturation timeline is a 2-3 week biological reality that no current product can compress.

3. **Stage 5 and Stage 7 coverage is purely symptomatic.** ORS and nutritional support keep calves alive but do not reduce pathology. Counting ORS toward pathology reduction is generous; a strict interpretation would set this near zero.

4. **Cryptosporidiosis is a disease where the 70% test may be structurally impossible with current science.** Forty years of research, 18 failed treatments, and the two best compounds in the world (EDI048 and AN7973) still do not achieve sterilizing cure. The disease biology --- epicellular niche, autoinfection, neonatal immune incompetence --- creates a pathology ceiling that no available portfolio can overcome.

### Gap Report

| Gap | Stage | What Would Fill It | Investment |
|-----|-------|-------------------|------------|
| **No sterilizing cure exists** | Stage 3 | EDI048 + AN7973 combination achieving >99.9% parasite clearance with no recrudescence | $500K+ in veterinary trials after licensing |
| **No immune potentiator validated** | Stage 4 | IL-22/ILC3 activator or IFN-gamma-inducing agent validated in neonatal calves against crypto | $50-100K (multiple candidates at $5-20K each) |
| **No mucosal repair intervention** | Stage 7 | A safe intervention that accelerates villous regeneration and closes the 34 kg growth deficit | Unknown --- EGF killed, no replacement identified |
| **Antiparasitic backbone not accessible** | Stage 3 | Novartis license for EDI048 veterinary rights; academic/global health partnership for AN7973 | Business development, not science |

## Step 6: Recommendation to Overwatch

The portfolio as currently constituted does not pass the 70% test. This is an honest result that reflects the state of the science, not a portfolio construction failure.

**What this means:**

1. **Do NOT inflate the numbers.** The Board was right: without the antiparasitic backbone, coverage is ~15%. With it, coverage reaches ~46% realistically, ~68% optimistically. Neither passes.

2. **This is a GAP REPORT, not a failure report.** The gaps are identified. The de-risk experiments are designed. The $108-142K investment resolves the three biggest unknowns.

3. **The structural reason this portfolio fails the 70% test:** Cryptosporidiosis is a disease where the rate-limiting barrier (epicellular niche + immune incompetence + autoinfection) has not been fully overcome by any compound in history. The 70% test failure reflects the frontier of the science, not the quality of the analysis.

4. **Recommendation:** Proceed to Anvil Phase 5 deliverables (evidence register and decision memo) with honest coverage numbers. Present to Cargill as a gap-closing program, not a solved portfolio. The de-risk experiments are the investment case.
