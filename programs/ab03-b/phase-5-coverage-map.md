## Step 2: The 70% Test

### Disease Stage Classification

| Disease Stage | Mechanism | Classification | Rationale |
|---------------|-----------|----------------|-----------|
| **Stage 1: Entry/Exposure** | Inhibitor administration | **NON-TRACTABLE** | The inhibitor IS the intended therapy (methane reduction). RHAS is a side effect. Dose management is veterinary practice, not a drug target. |
| **Stage 2: H2 Accumulation** | Dissolved H2 rises 5-40x | **TRACTABLE** | Pharmacological H2 disposal or H2 production reduction is the core drug target for AB03-B. |
| **Stage 3: Compensatory Failure** | Alternative sinks insufficient (<70% capture) | **TRACTABLE** | Enhancing compensatory sinks via novel electron acceptors, redox mediators, or engineered biology is the primary intervention approach. |
| **Stage 4: Acute Pathology** | NADH block, VFA shift, propionate deficit | **TRACTABLE** | THE rate-limiting bottleneck (NADH reoxidation). The direct drug target. Also includes downstream propionate deficit addressable by symptomatic treatments. |
| **Stage 5: Chronic Persistence** | Sustained productivity loss during inhibitor use | **TRACTABLE** | Requires continuous treatment delivery (bolus or daily additive). Addressable by the same interventions as Stages 2-4 if delivered continuously. |
| **Stage 6: Treatment Resistance** | All prior approaches failed | **TRACTABLE** | The portfolio's novel candidates (redox mediators, DIET, iron reduction) specifically circumvent the 4-constraint barrier (thermodynamics + cost + safety + spatial) that defeated all prior treatments. |
| **Stage 7: Continuous H2 Generation** | Stoichiometric byproduct of fermentation | **NON-TRACTABLE** | H2 production is inseparable from fermentation. ## Gap Analysis

### Why the Test Fails

1. **No candidate has any RHAS-specific efficacy data.** Zero. The entire portfolio is pre-experimental for the target disease. Every coverage estimate above is based on thermodynamic calculations, non-RHAS supporting data, and mechanism plausibility. This is honest but it means coverage is not "demonstrated" — it is "projected."

2. **The rate-limiting disease stages (2, 3, 4) have no validated interventions.** Stages 2-4 collectively represent 85% of tractable pathology. The best candidates for these stages (menadione, phloroglucinol, biochar) are all WOUNDED with fundamental mechanistic questions unresolved:
   - Menadione: catalytic shuttle or consumed vitamin? Single-lab data.
   - Phloroglucinol: conflicting in vivo data; inhibitor mismatch (chloroform vs 3-NOP).
   - Biochar DIET: 14 years without confirmation in the rumen.

3. **The coverage gap is NOT a candidate gap — it is a validation gap.** The portfolio has 13 wounded candidates addressing Stages 2-4. The problem is not "insufficient ideas" but "insufficient evidence." This is the expected state for a novel disease with zero prior treatments.

4. **Even the maximum coverage estimate (45.2%) is below 70% because individual candidates cannot eliminate entire disease stages.** RHAS is a thermodynamic cascade where partial H2 reduction produces partial (not complete) restoration of NADH kinetics. The FT curve's steepness helps — modest H2 reduction has disproportionate impact — but even perfect candidates at individual stages cannot claim 100% stage coverage.

The 70% test cannot pass until KE#1 is run and at least one candidate validates in RUSITEC under RHAS conditions. Specifically:

**The honest assessment: the 70% test is expected to fail at this pre-experimental stage. The portfolio structure is sound (it addresses all tractable stages with novel mechanisms), but coverage cannot be claimed until validation data exists. The KE#1 and Priority De-Risk Sequence are not optional — they are prerequisites for the 70% test.**
