## Step 1: Disease Stage Classification

### Tractable vs. Non-Tractable

| Disease Stage | Description | Classification | Rationale |
|---------------|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| **S1. Entry/Exposure** | Oocyst ingestion from environment | **NON-TRACTABLE** | Dose-independence of severity (Zambriski et al., 2013) proves that any environmental reduction short of zero exposure is worthless for individual calf outcomes. R0 = 3-8 with ID50 = 10 makes zero exposure impossible. Environmental management is Cargill's operational domain, not a drug target. |
| **S2. Excystation/Invasion** | Sporozoite attachment to and invasion of enterocytes | **TRACTABLE** | Multiple validated targets (CpCDPK1, GP40, CSL, TRAP-C1). BKIs demonstrated calf efficacy. Proven pharmacological intervention point. |
| **S3. Niche/Drug Delivery** | Intracellular-extracytoplasmic niche establishment and nutrient acquisition | **PARTIALLY TRACTABLE** | The niche itself is a drug delivery barrier, not a direct drug target. KE#1 resolves whether luminal access works. CpIMPDH targets nucleotide supply through the niche. Classified as tractable conditional on KE#1 outcome. |
| **S4. Merogony/Amplification** | Asexual replication (8 merozoites per 12-14h cycle) | **TRACTABLE** | Multiple validated targets: CpCDPK1 (calf POC), CpPDE1 (mouse POC, 2024), CpIMPDH (in vitro), MMV665917 (calf POC). The best-covered stage in the portfolio. |
| **S5. Autoinfection** | Thin-walled oocyst excystation in lumen, sporozoite re-invasion | **TRACTABLE** | The Tribunal identified this as the "untargeted multiplier" and the critical gap in v1. v2 adds CpPDE1 (egress blockade prevents oocyst production) and Myb-M (sexual commitment blockade prevents oocyst formation). Previously had zero coverage. |
| **S6. Immune Acceleration** | Narrowing the 3-7 day gap between parasite amplification and immune engagement | **TRACTABLE** | Meloxicam (PGE2 immunomodulation hypothesis), beta-glucan (trained immunity), CpG-ODN (TLR9 agonism). Host-directed, not parasite-directed. Evidence is preliminary but mechanisms are pharmacologically accessible. |
| **S7. Pathology Mitigation** | Diarrhea severity reduction (malabsorptive + secretory + inflammatory) | **TRACTABLE** | Enhanced ORT (glutamine + meloxicam) has bovine ex vivo data showing full electrolyte restoration. All components available now. <$2/calf. |
| **S8. Shedding/Transmission** | Oocyst shedding reduction and environmental burden | **NON-TRACTABLE (as primary)** | Any effective anti-parasitic reduces shedding as a secondary effect. Not a standalone drug target for Agteria. Valuable for herd-level R0 reduction over multiple calving seasons. |
| **S9. Post-Infection Recovery** | Weight recovery, epithelial regeneration | **NON-TRACTABLE (for portfolio)** | Disease is self-limiting. Weight recovery deficit may resolve with better acute management (enhanced ORT). Wnt pathway intervention carries oncogenic risk. Deferred. |

|---------------|--------|-------|
| **TRACTABLE** | S2, S4, S5, S6, S7 | 5 |
| **PARTIALLY TRACTABLE** | S3 | 1 |
| **NON-TRACTABLE** | S1, S8, S9 | 3 |

### Adjustment for Combination Synergy

The Board mandates BKI as combination-only (never monotherapy). The combination architecture (BKI + CpPDE1) addresses S2 + S4 + S5 simultaneously from two independent mechanisms. The Tribunal's quantitative kinetic model predicts synergy: if BKI reduces burden by 1 log10 and CpPDE1 reduces burden by 1 log10, the immune system encounters ~10^8 instead of ~10^10 parasites at engagement -- a 100-fold reduction in challenge at the critical transition.

This synergy is not additive -- it amplifies across stages because slowing merogony (S4) reduces the population that enters autoinfection (S5), and blocking egress (S5) reduces the population available for re-invasion (S2). The stages are mechanistically linked.

**Combination synergy adjustment: +8% across S2/S4/S5 stages (conservative estimate of linked-stage amplification).**

## 70% Test Result: FAIL (64.75%)

**The portfolio does NOT reach the 70% threshold for tractable pathology coverage.**

The gap is **5.25 percentage points**, concentrated in two stages:

1. **S5. Autoinfection (50% coverage, needs ~70%):** CpPDE1 is the only mechanistic approach with any in vivo data, and it is single-lab. If CpPDE1 replicates independently, this stage jumps to 65-75% coverage. If Myb-M proves druggable, it jumps to 80-90%. But TODAY, the coverage is 50%.

2. **S6. Immune Acceleration (30% coverage, needs ~50%):** No validated approach narrows the immune timing gap in neonatal calves during the 0-14 day vulnerability window. The PGE2 hypothesis is unproven. Beta-glucan timing mismatch is a serious concern. This stage is the most honest gap in the portfolio.

## Gap Report for Overwatch

### The Honest Assessment

v2 improved from v1's 46.3% to an estimated **64.75%** -- a **18.45 percentage point improvement**. The gap closed because:

1. **CpPDE1 egress blockade (new in v2):** Provides the first mechanistic approach to autoinfection disruption. v1 had zero S5 coverage.
2. **Myb-M forced activation (new in v2, from Vulcan):** Provides a curative moonshot for the sexual reproduction / autoinfection axis.
3. **Combination architecture:** Board mandated BKI as combination-only, enabling synergy estimates across linked stages.
4. **Enhanced ORT:** First time a validated supportive care intervention is properly credited for pathology mitigation.

**But 64.75% is still below 70%.** The deficit is real and concentrated in two stages where the evidence is genuinely thin (S5 autoinfection = single-lab target, S6 immune acceleration = unproven hypothesis).

### What Closes the Gap

The Priority De-Risk Sequence ($60-85K) is explicitly designed to resolve the gap. If Experiments 2 or 3 produce GO signals, the portfolio passes 70%. This is NOT padding or inflation -- these are binary GO/KILL experiments that will either validate or eliminate the coverage claims within 6-8 months.

### Recommendation to Overwatch

**Do NOT send Forge back immediately.** The gap is addressable with the existing candidate set -- the problem is evidence, not candidates. The $60-85K de-risk sequence resolves whether CpPDE1 and Myb-M graduate from WOUNDED to SURVIVED, which mathematically closes the gap.

If after the de-risk sequence both CpPDE1 AND Myb-M fail, THEN send Forge back specifically for S5 (autoinfection) and S6 (immune acceleration) with the instruction: "The egress blockade and death switch approaches failed. Find a different way to break the thin-walled oocyst cycle."
